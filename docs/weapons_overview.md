Derived metrics (per mounted copy; mounting N copies fires the system at rate `N/cooldown`, so it scales linearly and cancels in the per-space ratio):

- **DPS** = `damage / cooldown`
- **DPS/space** = `DPS / item-space` — damage-per-second efficiency per unit of item space
- **Range** = `speed × lifetime` — a projectile despawns after `lifetime` seconds; guided missiles cruise at `speed`

## Caveats
- **Ammo weapons** (missiles, mines) win on *burst* DPS but their *sustained* output is capped by ammo. Among unlimited (primary) weapons the DPS/space order is what matters for a long fight.
- **Mines** (`space_mine`, `prox_mine`) have near-zero `speed`, so their listed *range* is just how far they drift before expiring — they are area-denial dropped in place, not a firing range.
- **Guided missiles** home, so their *effective* reach exceeds the straight-line `speed × lifetime` shown here.
- **Turrets** trade space-efficiency for a single high-output auto-aiming mount: they rank low on DPS/space by design. If you are optimising damage-per-space, mount several small primaries instead.
- **Carrier bays** (`*_bay`) are omitted from both tables — they launch escort ships rather than projectiles, so they have no direct DPS or projectile range; their value is the escorts they field.
